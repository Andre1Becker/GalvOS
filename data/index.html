📋 Tuning Procedure

① Set scan size — reduce until circle begins to distort, then back 10%
12K mode: up to ~15° optical  |  30K mode: max ~8° optical (ILDA rated)
② Check axis polarity (A4)
"Y" label must appear at top, "X" label at right. If reversed → invert the signal at the galvo driver card. In GalvOS: Config → Calibration → Invert X / Invert Y.
③ Adjust Y damping (A2 — lower-right corner of inner square)
Increase low-freq damping until a small overshoot appears at the corner. Overshoot length ≈ 2/3 height of reference marker. Then adjust high-freq damping to minimize that overshoot length. Finally reduce low-freq damping to tuck overshoot back to a dot. ⚠ Undershoot (corner gap) is harder to detect — avoid going too far.
④ Adjust Y servo gain (A1 — circle vs. inner square top/bottom)
Circle top/bottom must touch the inner square edges. Too small → increase gain, repeat ③. Too large → decrease gain, repeat ③.
⑤ Adjust X damping (A2 — upper-right corner of inner square)
Same procedure as step ③ but for the X axis. Do NOT re-adjust Y axis after this step.
⑥ Adjust X servo gain (circle vs. inner square left/right)
Circle left/right must touch inner square edges. Result: circle touches square at exactly 4 points and is perfectly round. If not round → iterate ⑤/⑥ carefully, then re-verify ③/④.
⑦ Adjust image size (A5)
Reset size to normal. Adjust X/Y amplitude so outer square fills the usable projection area. In GalvOS: Config → Calibration → X Gain / Y Gain sliders.
⑧ Adjust DC offset (A6 — center cross must be at screen center)
Move X/Y offset until cross is centered. Prefer physical galvo rotation for large offsets. In GalvOS: Config → Calibration → X Offset / Y Offset.
⑨ Blanking test (B1/B2 — two horizontal lines with center gap)
Gap must be symmetric around the vertical tick mark. Adjust blanking damping until gap is centered. Adjust blanking servo gain until the two line segments appear to converge at the tick when extrapolated.
⑩ Speed dots (B3/B4 — row of 6 dots, decreasing brightness)
Dot 5 (5th from left) must be barely visible. Dot 6 (rightmost) must be fully blanked. Adjust blanking DC offset until this threshold is met. In GalvOS: blanking is digital via 6N137 — dot behavior reflects the optical blanking transition of the laser modules.
Expected result: Circle perfectly round, touching inner square at top, bottom, left, right. Corners of inner square sharp (no overshoot or undershoot). Cross centered. Outer square fills screen area. Gap in blanking lines centered and symmetric.
